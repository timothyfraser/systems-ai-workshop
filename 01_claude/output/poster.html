Networks & Resilience

When a shock hits a city — a flood, an outage, an outbreak — why do some communities bounce back while others stall? This talk traces resilience to the structure of the networks beneath a city: who is connected to whom, where the hubs are, and which links matter most when things break.

What you'll take away

  • How network shape (hubs, path length, clustering) drives how shocks spread
  • Simple measures that flag fragile points before they fail
  • Where social infrastructure fits into engineering for resilience
